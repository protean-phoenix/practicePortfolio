About Me

My journey towards the sciences began with an interest in biology, which I pursued as a major in university. I enjoyed the aspect of always being able to learn new things that came with the sciences. However, when I had a chance to participate in biological research, I found that wet lab work wasn't for me.

I decided to change my major to computer science, which interested me for its engineering aspects. I always liked the idea of being able to build and modify programs. The fact that programs are intangible and can be made anywhere also interested me.

Part way through my progress toward a computer science degree I had the desire to work on real world projects both in groups and alone. In order to get practice working in groups and on projects I attended learning fuze which taught me how to use many tools and technologies related to web development. Now I am ready to bring what I've learned to the work place!
